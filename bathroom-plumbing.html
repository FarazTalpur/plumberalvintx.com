Frequently Asked Questions

To prevent bathroom pipes from clogging, avoid flushing non-degradable items like tissues or sanitary products, use drain covers to catch hair and soap debris, and regularly clean drains using a mixture of baking soda and vinegar followed by hot water.

If your faucet is leaking, start by identifying the type of faucet and turning off the water supply. Then, carefully disassemble the faucet, inspect it for worn parts, such as washers or seals, and replace them as necessary. If the issue persists, consider consulting a plumber.

To increase water pressure in your shower, first check for any mineral buildup on the showerhead and clean it with vinegar. Ensure there are no leaks in the plumbing and that the main water valve is fully open. If necessary, consider installing a shower pump or consulting with a plumber for further assessment.

A constantly running toilet can be due to a faulty flapper, a float that's too high, or an issue with the fill valve. Check the flapper for wear and replace if necessary. Adjust the float level to ensure it shuts off the valve properly. Inspect the fill valve and replace if there's a malfunction.

For a slow-draining bathroom sink, try removing any visible obstructions and clear the P-trap. Use a plunger or a plumbing snake to clear deeper clogs. Regular maintenance with a mix of baking soda and vinegar can help keep drains clear.
