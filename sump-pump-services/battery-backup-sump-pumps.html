Frequently Asked Questions

A battery backup sump pump is an additional pump system that operates using a battery, providing emergency power in case of a primary pump failure or electricity outage. It is essential for protecting your basement from flooding during power outages or when the main sump pump fails.

The duration a battery backup sump pump can run during a power outage depends on the battery capacity and the pump's usage rate. Typically, a fully charged battery can last for about 7 to 10 hours of continuous pumping, or up to several days if the pump only operates intermittently.

Most batteries for backup sump pumps need replacement every 3 to 5 years. However, it is important to regularly check the battery's condition and perform maintenance according to the manufacturer's guidelines to ensure optimal performance.

Battery backup sump pumps are designed to handle moderate to heavy rains. However, if you're expecting exceptionally severe weather conditions, it's important to ensure your battery and pump system are in top condition, and you might need a system rated for higher capacity.

While some people may choose to install a battery backup sump pump themselves, it is typically recommended to hire a professional to ensure proper installation and optimal operation. Incorrect installation can lead to pump failure during critical moments.
