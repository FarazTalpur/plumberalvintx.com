Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is a device used to remove accumulated water in a sump basin, usually found in a basement. It helps prevent flooding and protects your home from water damage by channeling water away from the foundation.

It is recommended to test your sump pump every three months and before the rainy season. Regular testing ensures it's functioning correctly when needed.

Routine maintenance includes checking for any debris in the sump pit, ensuring the pump is working by pouring water into the pit, and inspecting the discharge pipe for obstructions. An annual professional inspection is also advised.

Sump pumps generally last between 7 to 10 years. Longevity depends on factors such as frequency of use, maintenance routine, and the quality of the pump.

If your sump pump stops working, check for power issues, ensure the float switch is not obstructed, and inspect for any clogs. If problems persist, contact a professional to avoid potential damage or flooding.
